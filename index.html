02 — About me

The long way around

Chemistry is the thing I keep coming back to. Not because it was assigned to me — because at some point the problems stopped feeling like homework and started feeling like puzzles I wanted to be better at. Most of my studying is aimed at competitions, and I'd be lying if I said college wasn't part of the reason. But the studying itself is the part I'd do anyway.

The rest of my time splits between things that make something and things that don't. Robotics and the nonprofit make things. Basketball behind the garage, the saxophone, whatever I've decided to bake that week — those don't make anything, and that's exactly the point of them.

I made this site because I wanted a place that's mine, that isn't a profile on someone else's platform. It'll change as I do.

A nonprofit bringing STEM opportunities — with an emphasis on robotics — to students across the St. Louis region through hands-on experiences, funding, and mentoring. I help run it, and the camps we put on are how we fund the work.
